---
title: "Understanding vs Knowing"
date: 2026-05-24
---

Knowing something is what I would call **fake intelligence**. It is the ability to recall information. But the essential skill is to be able to use that information in a meaningful way, and so, **understanding** it.

Understanding is about the **why**: considering the outer layer around the information. To go even further, it is about being able to connect information and use it in different contexts.

The more you understand things, the broader connections you can make. But wait, this yielded data structure seems familiar... Of course, it is a **brain**!
