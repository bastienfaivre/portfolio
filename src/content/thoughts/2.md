---
title: "The Grammarly effect"
date: 2026-06-09
---

Most of my digital activity is in English, and I've been using Grammarly for the past two years. I recently realized the tool has fixed most of the recurring mistakes I used to make.

I think that instant, direct feedback is the key: our brain learns to predict the incoming error tag as we type the wrong pattern, and eventually it directly instructs us to use the correct one. On top of that, we tend to reuse the same patterns in our writing, which amplifies the effect over time.

I should pay more attention to whether such a correction paradigm occurs in other daily actions and where I could potentially integrate it.
